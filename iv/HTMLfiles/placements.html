  TCS hires graduates and final year students from BCA and BSC(IT/Maths) and the top performers are assigned IGNITE profile and others are assigned Smart Hiring profile. In the IGNITE program, you are most likely to get a development project you will also get an MCA degree from Sastra University. Round 1(TCS NQT Exam): So in my case, the 1st round was the TCS NQT exam in which I was asked questions on Maths, Reasoning, and Verbal. The duration was 3 hours and MCQ-based. The next day we had to go for giving the programming and IT exam for NQT.The programming questions were fairly easy. After 1 month the results were announced I got 80% in my NQT aptitude and 75% in my programming and I was sent the link for the technical interview. Technical Interview: The technical interview was completely based on the resume. My resume had a couple of projects on web development and also had On job training experience so I was continuously asked questions on web development, JavaScript, C++, DBMS,C ,software engeenering and others it lasted for 30 mins approx and it was like a rapid-fire round they even asked me to write the code for string triangle and to convert binary number to decimal on my notepad and asked me to find the output of a program given by the panel. the panel has 3-4 interviewers. Well, I would like to say one thing that if you don’t have any projects then the interviewer will ask you in deep questions about OOPS and RDBMS . (So its completely fine to go without a project but if you add a thing in your resume which you cannot explain then you will get trapped). After 15 days I got my offer letter, and they initiated the BGC process.

Shreya Sharan
(Deloitte, Cognizant, DE Shaw)
  For Deloitte, there were 2 rounds .The first round was the online assessment that dealt with the reasoning, quantative aptitude,and English section . The level was moderate. I guess if you have basic knowledge and the concepts you can surely clear it and the exam was conducted through amcat .Then the second round was the interview round where basically they test 3C (Confidence, concepts and conversation skill)so they started with the introduction then some questions related to leadership skills ,teamwork ,success etc and for the technical part they will check your concepts .First they ask your favourite subject then questions like joins ,oops etc where basically the questions revolved around your resume .Then there was some coding questions only to check concepts like prime number ,palindrome etc. Then the turing point was case study .One can surely turns the table if they do good in case study they check your thought process to deal with situations they put you into. They ask about the projects you have done .Since I had no project work so I was asked about the question related to computer skills If you have proper concepts of the skills mentioned in your resume and a good conversation skills you can definitely crack the interview.
For Cognizant, the process was same there were 2 rounds.The only difference was in the interview they didn't ask for any case study .In place of case study they will ask you puzzels .they will provide you a minute or two to solve the puzzel and answer them.You don't have to only answer but to explain how you solved them.
For DE SHAW ,here comes the boss .Since they are paying you so much they are looking for only the best .The questions level of De shaw is much more higher than other companies. The first round was the online assessment it had questions from quantative aptitude, data interpretation, reasoning,comprehension, and some question from computer and technologies. If you clear the section then immediately you have a round of asynchronous video interview in which you are provided with 3 topics and you have to record each topic for 3 minutes .and if you clear that then you have 2 technical round of interview and 1 hr round ..There interview level is very high .I don't know much about the interview process as I was called for the interview but couldn't attend due to health issues. Just be confident enough for the interview and start practicing for the online assessment from now and if you have poor conversation skill plz work on that because I have seen people being rejected due to there speaking skills. All the best for future.

Govinda Kumar
(SAP, Deloitte, Cognizant, Wipro, Infosys)
  Hey, I am from CAPP (2018-21). I have been selected in 5 companies; Deloitte, Wipro, Infosys, Cognizant and SAP. Selection process of all companies were much similar except of SAP. Like Deloitte had first round of assessment test in which questions were asked in three sections ENGLISH, QUANTS reasoning and LOGICAL reasoning. This same pattern was followed in Infosys and Cognizant, but Wipro had one more phase if you clear the first of this test then you will be taken immediately to phase 2 where you will get a situation and asked to write about it, this phase 2 thing is also taken in Cognizant. After this first round you will be taken to interview round, Deloitte, Infosys and Cognizant have one and only round of interview (tech. + HR). But Wipro have two rounds of interview; first as Technical and second as HR.
Questions asked to me in interview round were of good level in Deloitte but in Infosys, Wipro and Cognizant it was very much basics. Few I remember are: Introduction, Interest and hobbies, challenges faced in life and how did I overcome, and most importantly regarding PROJECT. I personally recommend you to do projects somehow it favours the interview in your side.
After few days you receive mails stating you are selected in the company.
ALL THE VERY BEST FOR YOUR FUTURE!! I believe in you, you can do whatever you want to.
